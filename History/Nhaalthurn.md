# Nhaalthurn

**Nhaalthurn** — *the Turning Breath* — is the oldest continuously observed holiday in [[Korlornium]], marking the passage from the final night of [[Salaale (Quiet)]] into the first dawn of [[Nhaale (Inhale)]]. It is both an ending and a beginning: a single breath held between the death of one year and the birth of the next.

## Observance

The holiday spans two halves, divided at midnight.

### The Quiet Evening (Salaale 80)
Beginning at sundown on the last day of [[Salaale (Quiet)|Salaale]], a silence descends across observing communities. Fires are kept low. Taverns close or dim their lights. Conversations are abandoned mid-sentence. It is considered deeply ill-mannered — and in some traditions, outright unlucky — to speak aloud during the Quiet Evening.

The silence is not mourning. It is listening. The belief, rooted in older traditions, is that the world itself pauses to exhale before it can breathe again, and those who are still enough may hear it.

### The First Morning (Nhaale 1)
At the first light of Nhaale 1, the silence breaks. Tradition holds that music should be the first sound of the new year — not words, not drums, but melody. In villages across [[Korlornium]], musicians position themselves to play at dawn: from rooftops, bridges, the bows of ships. The music does not have to be coordinated or rehearsed. The cacophony of a dozen unrelated songs rising together at sunrise is considered more joyful than any formal performance.

Families gather at dawn to hear the first notes, and it is customary to hum or sing whatever tune finds you first — even if you don't know the words.

## Regional Variations

- In [[Stormveil]], the tradition leans into the natural quiet of the forest. Many residents spend the Quiet Evening walking the woods alone, and the dawn music is typically performed on reed instruments made from [[Stormwood]].
- In [[Duskwind Landing]], sailors observe the Quiet Evening at sea when possible, believing that the ocean's silence on that night is especially profound. The morning celebration is loud and communal at the docks.
- In [[Dithoria City]], the silence is largely ceremonial and frequently broken — the upper classes host wordless dinner parties, communicating through written notes and dramatic gestures. The morning celebration involves hired orchestras.

## Origins

The holiday predates recorded history in most of [[Korlornium]]. The oldest known reference appears in fragments of [[Yygarissil]]'s scripture, describing the gods themselves going silent on the eve of the world's second breath. Whether this is literal theology or poetic tradition is debated.

Some scholars argue Nhaalthurn originated among an older people who understood the calendar not as a human invention but as a living cycle — the world breathing in, holding, breathing out, and holding again. The holiday, in this view, marks the moment the world chooses to breathe in once more.

## In the Current Year

Nhaalthurn of 12004 fell while the party known as [[The Salty Bitches]] was still operating out of [[Duskwind Landing]], early in their adventures. Whether any of them observed it is unrecorded.

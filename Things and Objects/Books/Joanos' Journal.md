# Joanos' Journal
Most of the journal is stained, worn and severely water damaged. Pages are torn but a few remain readable.

## Entry 40: Date Unknown
> They will call me enslaved. Broken. But they misunderstand what _freedom_ truly is.
> 
> In death, the bindings of fear fell away. In life, I served captains and codes and flags. Now, I serve something greater—[[Zora Meadowlark|Zora]], who sees through time like wind through sails. She showed me what lies beneath all things. Magic. Memory. Power.
> 
> I must hold strong. I must prove myself worthy. For only those with great will may wield such powers.



## Entry 55: Date Unknown
> The salt sticks to everything out here. Even my thoughts.
> 
> [[Yannis Trask|Yannis]] is... efficient. Brutal. He doesn’t question Zora’s mission, so I find him tolerable. Our goal is simple, if not easily achieved—find the remnants of the ancient city that once lay buried beneath what men now call [[Duskwind Landing]].
> 
> But the deeper we sail into the delta, the more we feel it—_tremors_. Not of the sea, but from the deep bedrock beneath. Zora believes the city still stirs, that something beneath the salt and sediment still _breathes_.
> 
> She calls it Pre-Karthish. A name forgotten, carved into obsidian tablets lost beneath the Scar's edge. The land fell there once, but why? Calamity? I find the Caldirthian tales of [[The Great Wave]] hog water. There must be more... more power to unlock. 
> 
> We’ve found fragments already—runes older than [[Fowlmen]]. Glyphs that whisper when the moon is low. If I focus too long, my vision bleeds and I see through time.
> 
> This is no mere expedition. We are standing on the cracked crust of a god’s grave.
>

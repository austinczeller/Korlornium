# Vel'Korr
**Player**: Ben
**Class**: Rouge
**Species**: [[Drow]]

![[Velkorr.png]]

**Short Description**
* Inspired by Captain Jack Sparrow
* Spent time at sea and is trying to assemble a crew

**Description**
[[Drow]] pirate-like male with large pirate hat and two scimitars, and two crossbows strapped to his side. He is charming and always ready to make a deal and manipulate others to benefit himself. However, as he has been traveling with [[Faefi]], she has softened him up and seems as though he might not only think about himself. He is a caring owner of [[Beak]] who never is as useful as Velkorr wishes. His personable energy makes him sort of the leader of the crew.
Budding bromance with [[Kel]] and rival to [[Thorne]].

**Personal Quest**
* Gather a crew

## Backstory
Although [[Drow]] are not rare on the surface they are still fairly uncommon. My blood kin still prefer the dark and warmth of the underdark.

I was just a child when I was found upon the surface world, though no one knew how I came to be here. Abandoned as an infant, my small, swaddled body was found in the woods near the western edge of a quiet village called [[Scar's Embrace]], nestled in a cove at the southern point of [[Korlornium]]'s western land [[Caldrith]]. The one who found me wasn’t just a random poor peasant or a rich noble or a god fearing priest, but a red-skinned Tiefling named [[Varnak]] the Red, leader of a morally gray band of pirates.

[[Varnak]]’s group, known as [[The Red Talons]] had come ashore to resupply and spend their hard earned gold in the local taverns and brothels. During their time upon land, [[Varnak]] and the ships wizard, an old gray haired human named [[Joanos]], were wondering around the outskirts of [[The Scar]]'s Embrace discussing future plans for the crew when [[Varnak]]'s raven [[Beak]] came to him with news. A Dark Elf babe had been seen left under a Godswood tree. 

[[Varnak]], having once been a slave to an aristocratic house, had his own reasons for turning to a life of robbing the wealthy. He saw in me an opportunity: a child with the potential for greatness, though perhaps not greatness as most would define it. The band of rouges had a strict code: we only stole from the rich, the corrupt, or the unjust. Our heists were calculated, our targets carefully chosen, and our thieves, efficient. But our compassion was limited, and our sense of justice... flexible. 

They took me in, raised me on the seas, taught me to fight, to charm, and to lie with the ease of a seasoned con artist, taught me to sail, to drink and to gamble. I grew up under [[Varnak]]’s tutelage, honing my skills as a rogue. My first weapon was a hand crossbow, a tool of finesse and precision, but my true talent lay in the dance of the duel. Soon, I wielded two slightly curved scimitars, spinning them through the air with grace and deadly accuracy. I learned how to move with the swiftness of the wind, leaving foes disoriented and bleeding in my wake, all while using my innate drow abilities of darkness and fairy fire to blind and confuse our enemies.

Though [[The Red Talons]] were just a bunch of criminals and misfits, we shared a bond, a strange family formed by a sense of loyalty and the harsh realities of our trade. The most notable among our resources was [[Varnak]]’s obsidian crow-shaped figurine—[[Beak]]—a wondrous magical artifact that could transform into a crow and scout ahead for the bandits and targets. I quickly became close with the bird, who had a sharp, calculating mind and over the years [[Beak]] and I became surrogate siblings of sorts. The tiefling leader saw in me not just a skillful thief, but a rising star—charming, confident, and with an unmistakable charisma that drew others to him. 

However, after decades of successful raids adventures and sailing, our fates would change when we made one fateful decision. After pulling in to port of [[Frostcrag Glade]] a wintry town in the icy foothills of the range of the [[Agite Mountains]] that survived and prospered from the trade relations they shared with the mysterious mountain Dwarves of the area. 

In pursuit of riches and thrills, and a very large bounty, we accepted a Quest from the towns leader to take out a powerful Elven sorcerer named [[Viros]], who we had heard was kidnapping and experimenting on the people from town. We rested, resupplied and headed off to the lone sea tower we were told [[Viros]] had taken as his own. But we underestimated our prey. [[The Red Talons]] sailed straight into a trap. 

It started small. Just a slight unexpected change in the wind. That's all it took for [[Varnak]] to realize something wasn't right. Shouting for us to get in to our ready positions, just as fast as the first rain drop fell, a huge wave stuck our port side and toppled some of the crew over the rails to the watery depths below. As we battled through the magical storm fighting and sailing better than any crew that had come before, our ship was taking more damage than she had ever seen before and with our wizard, [[Joanos]] exhausting his magic trying to counter the magical storm and protect the ship from falling apart. He failed to sense what was coming for us from below. 

As we approached the lone dark ocean tower, lowering the life rafts to onboard to make the final stretch to [[Viros]]. A tentacle the size of our main shaft came crashing down and smashed into the ship, damaging it beyond repair. Knocking Myself, [[Varnak]] and [[Joanos]] in to a raft, we cut the safety lines and crashed fully in to the ocean and started paddling to make [[Viros]] pay. With [[Joanos]] concentration broken and protection spells gone, the tentacled creature struck again and again crushing and wrapping our up ship, our home and dragging it down to the depths back to the hells it called home.

Our hope rekindled a little when we saw from the wreckage a few life crafts had made it out alive and were following behind us. That hope was short lived though as a huge fire ball came raining down from the top of the tower and crashed in to one of our few survivors. As it hit, it lit up the area like a sunny day and the sound of screams hit our ears and smell of flesh hit our nose. And then another. And another... His magic tore through us like a Mithril scimitar through warm butter. We stood no chance. 

Only two boats managed to make it to the shore of the lone tower. Five survivors in total made it off our ship alive. Five out of 20. I lost a lot of good brothers and sisters that day. 

With nowhere to go but up we headed up the tower not only for the bounty now but also revenge. As we climbed up and up we lost another, our chef a half orc named [[Torag]], he lost his head as he blindly ran down a corridor not seeing the very obvious trap that had been laid out for us. Not until we got to to the top, up to the laboratory did we realize the severity of what we were in for. Humans, elves, dwarves all manner of the races from the land even a [[Fowlmen]] from [[Siroth]]. All strapped up dying or dead, covered in ancient runic symbols. (Looking back now I think it must have been a summoning circle of some kind maybe to bring in that creature that took down our ship, but alas I will never know). 

As we approached the door to [[Viros]] we heard an almighty crack and felt the air go static as the door came flying off it's hinges and crashed in to our last crew mate, our lookout, a half elf named [[Astra]] and proceeded to flatten her against the opposite wall with such force the door never fell over. Another one gone. 

All hell broke loose then, Me dropping darkness in the room, [[Joanos]] throwing lightning bolts and [[Varnak]] charging in with his huge great sword held high hoping to strike [[Viros]]' head clean from his shoulders. He did. Or would have if the image he had seen was truly [[Viros]], but it was just a duplicate image. The image puffed away in cloud of smoke as a crackling laugh came from above and a voice called out "Fools you can not stop what is already in motion, I hope you enjoy what comes next" as we looked up we heard an impossibly loud screech as the top of the tower just vanished in to thin air and a tentacle came crashing down in to the room. Destroying all the walls and several of the top floors of the tower. Down we fell through the rubble smashing and crashing off the debris. 

Finally landing back in laboratory, I had come out of the ordeal not too badly injured considering what we had been through. Just a broken arm. I cannot say the same for my companions though, [[Joanos]] was missing an eye and shattered both legs and [[Varnak]] had landed on a wooden support impaling him completely through the stomach. His time was up and he knew it. As the dust started to settled, [[Varnak]] gave me his last command. The Tiefling, though mortally wounded, his life blood leaking out, he reached out and handed me the crow figurine. With a raspy voice, [[Varnak]] told me, “Take [[Beak]]... Let her guide you.” and with that he closed his eyes forever.

A shadow passed over us as the mouth of the monstrous creature passed by a hole in the wall and climbed up the tower with its gigantic tentacles wrapping around the tower a squeezing trying to rip the tower in two, rushing to find [[Joanos]] he lay crippled and weak he pulled me in close and whispered "it wasn't supposed to be like this" Then a flash of light...

I awoke on the rocky shore with waves lapping at my feet. Dazed, confused and in quite a lot of pain now the adrenaline of battle had lapsed. Sitting up I could see the remains of the dark tower the top half had been ripped off and toppled in to the sea and the creature no longer in sight. And there I fell in to a deep deep sleep.

Awaking an unknown amount of time later, I managed to stand and find the strength to call in Beak. Now, with her in my possession, I realized her true power, I could hear her thoughts and her mine, I could see through her eyes. She became my most valuable tool and my only friend left. At first I used to her to scout the ruins of the tower but had no luck with finding any survivors. Then she led me back to the town of [[Frostcrag Glade]]. Where I was patched up back to health and given half the bounty I was promised as [[Viros]] still lived.

With [[Beak]], my only friend left she tried to guide me as I escaped the chaos, of what had just happened. But she could only do so much the pain of losing my whole family took it's toll on me, I dulled my pain with drink and substances falling into a pit of despair trying to find a reason to go on.

After years of wondering lost and doing small jobs of protection and smuggling to earn enough to drink I have started to heal from the wounds of my old life with my charm and magnetic personality I have earned myself the reputation of a charming rogue, a swashbuckler who could steal your heart as easily as your purse. My skill with my twin scimitars, hand crossbows and innate abilities have made me a deadly adversary in combat, and my wit, confidence, and flair for theatrics made me a star wherever I went. With [[Beak]] always watching from above, I became a master at reading people, manipulating situations, and getting in and out of places where others would fail. Though my motivations remain unclear and confusing to many, I am driven by a desire for both revenge and success. I seek to right the wrongs of my past, to build an empire of my own, and to one day kill the one who destroyed my family, making them pay for their hubris. But whether my journey will be one of redemption, power, or something darker remains to be seen.

I have arrived in a small village of [[Duskwind Landing]], and have heard of a wizard that might be causing the disease known as sea rot. If I can take him out there may be some reward. There is an odd bunch of people in this Tavern that may come in handy for helping take out this wizard. A strange Tortle and his pet rat are going around handing out leaflets promising power, maybe this is a way to success??

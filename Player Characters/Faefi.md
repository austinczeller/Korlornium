# Faefi

**Player**: Laura
**Class**: Cleric
**Species**: [[Aasimar]]
![[Faffie.png]]
**Short Description**
Young [[Aasimar]] who seems lost. She has been traveling with [[Vel'Korr]] for a while now. They joked about once being romantically intimate, and nobody is sure if that is true. She doesn't take shit from anyone and has a good heart, which often is reality check for [[Vel'Korr]]'s antics.


**Personal Quest**
- Find a purpose in life


They used to say I was born from the light of the stars and the fury of the storm. I never knew my parents—only the cold stone walls of the [[Abbey of the Silver Veil]], nestled high in the [[Agite Mountains]]. The wind was sharp there, the kind that cuts through wool and will alike, but I never minded. The sisters told me I never cried as a baby, not even when the cold bit my skin. They said it was a sign of divine purpose.

  

Maybe they were right. Maybe not.

  

The abbey was a place of peace, of rules, of reflection. I was none of those things.

  

Don’t get me wrong—I loved the place. I loved the harmony of chanted prayers echoing through the halls at dawn, the warm bread we baked in the morning, and the hush that fell over the gardens at twilight. 

  

But rules? 

  

Structure? 

  

Silence?

  

 That was always harder for me.

  

My magic started showing early, and it never did what I wanted. Wild, untamed, chaotic. One moment I was meditating quietly in the chapel; the next, the altar candles exploded in radiant flame. I once sneezed during vespers and summoned a glowing badger into the prayer hall. The sisters tried to help me contain it. I tried too—but I was always a crackle away from catastrophe.

  

Except for [[Elyra|Mother Elyra]]. She was the abbey's leader, a stern but kind woman who saw something in me no one else could. She never tried to cage the storm in me. She tried to teach it to dance. I loved her like a mother. She treated me like a daughter.

  

But love doesn't stop the tide.

  

One night, when the stars blinked out and the wind stopped howling

  

It started with a low hum that turned to screaming. The dead came first—shambling up the mountain pass, hollow-eyed and rotting. Then came the fire, and the storm, and him—("[[Viros]]" his name I learned later) a sorcerer cloaked in shadow, his hands soaked in the blood of the fallen. He wasn't there to conquer. He came to erase. The abbey, the people, the purpose—it was all nothing to him.

  

He took them. As many as he could. Including [[Elyra]].

  

I remember the moment she vanished—her hand slipping from mine in a blast of dark energy, her eyes wide with something I couldn't name. Then it was over. Only smoke, blood, and silence remained.

  

I buried the few bodies I could find beneath the snow.

  

Then I came down from the mountains for the first real time in my life

  

I stumpbled across the town of [[Frostcrag Glade]] but they had no real answers, just whispers. They said Viros was last seen heading toward an old sea tower up the [[Ice Coast]]. I followed. For days I trekked north through sleet and salt wind, until I found the tower or what was left of it.

  

The top half had fallen into the sea, and a broken pirate ship lay smashed against the cliffs below. I was ready to mourn again. But fate, it seems, wasn't done with me yet.

  

There was one survivor, well two actually. [[Vel'Korr]]. A rouge pirate a cocky, drow with a simitar too big for his boots and a grin that didn’t know when to quit and his strange raven [[Beak]]. [[Vel'Korr]]said he and his crew had taken on a job come to rescue the prisoners and challenge [[Viros]] directly. But [[Viros]] summoned something... wrong. Tentacles the size of trees, black as abyssal ink, tore the tower apart. Only Vel'kor survived.

  

He was reckless. Loud. Arrogant. And utterly helpless without someone to watch his back. So, of course, I took him under mine.

  

We made a pact that day—[[Viros]] had to pay. For the abbey. For Elyra. For the dead.

  

Vel'kor fights for glory and justice, I think—though he’d never say it out loud. Me? I fight because there’s a storm inside me that won’t rest until it’s done. Until he's gone

  

And until then?

  

I'm his guardian. He’s my chaos. Together, we’re going to find [[Viros]].

  

And when we do…

  

Stars help him.

  

And if Velkor gets himself killed, I’m going to drag him back just so I can yell at him.

  

We’ll find [[Viros]].

  

One way or another.

### Roleplay Goals:

This scene’s purpose is to:

- Introduce Zora’s **charisma and motive** without revealing who she is yet.
    
- Give the players information about the **storm**, **Nilah**, and the **Gate Keys** indirectly.
    
- Let clever PCs detect something off (Insight, Arcana, Religion, or Perception).
    
- Foreshadow Zora’s philosophy — that _freedom_, even dangerous freedom, is worth the cost.
### Dialogue Beats

**Zora (as Mira)**:

> “The sky is trembling tonight, don’t you think? My sister says it’s the breath of Parsus, calling the faithful to witness her power. But I think… it’s something older.”

If the party mentions Nilah:

> “Ah, the storm dragon. The guardian of the heights. They say she’s been here since before the Scar, older than the gods who name her protector. I wonder if she tires of guarding secrets she’ll never use.”

If they mention the storm’s danger:

> “You can’t reach the heavens without walking through a storm. That’s what faith is, isn’t it?”

If the conversation turns to the gods or Dithoria:

> “Dithoria? I’ve heard whispers. A god asleep beneath the waves, yes? Some say his dreams are what shake the tides. If he suffers, should we not wake him?”

### What Terrance Might Reveal Under Interrogation

(You can release these in layers depending on rolls, tactics, or how long they keep him restrained.)

**Level 1: Easy to Extract (with even minor intimidation/persuasion)**

- He _didn’t hire_ the doppelgangers directly—someone else arranged that.
    
- He received “advice” and “support” from a mysterious benefactor connected to Heaven’s Hole (Zora’s agents).
    
- He _knows_ the attack was staged to pin blame on Heaven’s Hole, but pretends it was “an accident” that went wrong.
    

**Level 2: Moderate (DC 15–17 Persuasion/Intimidation)**

- He’s been promised a seat of power in Stormveil once Numa is discredited and Heaven’s Hole “put in its place.”
    
- The benefactor’s name is **Zora**, who he only knows as “the lady of the Bluffs.”
    
- He has correspondences (coded letters or contracts) hidden at his home/office tying him to Zora.
    

**Level 3: Hard (DC 18–20, or leverage like threatening to drag him before the mob)**

- He _knows_ Zora is researching the Fey pool and wants something from Heaven’s Hole she can’t get otherwise.
    
- He was supposed to make Stormveil dependent on her aid after the city destabilized.
    
- He’s terrified of being abandoned by his allies now that the plan failed.

### Public Perception Challenge

Terrance’s loyalists (wealthy merchants, some logging bosses, nervous citizens) don’t immediately believe he’s guilty. You can frame this as a **social skill challenge**:

- **Setup:** The town square is packed. Numa’s wounded but alive. Terrance is restrained, shouting insults. The people murmur, undecided.
    
- **Goal:** Convince the crowd Terrance is a traitor and rally them behind Numa (or the revolution).
    
- **Mechanics (3 successes before 2 failures):**
    
    - **Persuasion:** Calmly explain Terrance’s treachery and ties to outside forces.
        
    - **Intimidation:** Threaten Terrance into exposing himself with his own words.
        
    - **Deception:** Spin a more dramatic version of events if needed.
        
    - **Performance:** Rally the crowd with a rousing speech (Picasso’s union rhetoric could fit here).
        
    - **Insight:** Read the crowd, find which points sway them most.
        

---

### Terrance’s Racist Outburst

Once the players make progress, Terrance loses his composure:

- He screams that “the swamp filth of Heaven’s Hole” are corrupting Stormveil.
    
- He calls Numa “a savage, not fit to lead.”
    
- He blames the “vermin millers” for undermining Stormveil’s traditions.
    

This is a turning point—if the party steers the moment right, the public _sees his mask slip_ and rallies against him. If they fail, his words could stir up prejudice and unrest, sowing longer-term division (which Zora benefits from).

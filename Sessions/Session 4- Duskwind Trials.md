## Recap
> Last session [[Picasso]] and [[Soffalie]] met up with the party as they were resting near the entrance of a underground cavern. There were plenty of symbols of [[Ulthagos]] on banners and crests in the entryway. They were greeted by a playful glowing orb which led them to a larger room where a [[Spectator]] awaited. After a brief and sassy conversation battle commenced and the [[Spectator]] was defeated. The group noted large snake-like bones on the ground but proceeded into the lit library that the [[Spectator]] was guarding. In this room the party met [[Zora Meadowlark]] who appeared frightened at first but then creepily smiled revealing a long tongue and teleported out as soon as the party became suspicious of her. The party scoured the library and found some books about [[Ulthagos]], a box of beans, sneezing dust, and a book (ASK GROUP IF THEY REMEMBER WHAT ITEMS THEY GOT). As they were searching they began to feel the earth quake and quickly exited the cavern. As they emerged they witnessed a wave of destruction cascading over the [[Driftwood Forest]]. [[Soffalie]] expressed her anger and wants to know who is responsible.

As the rumbling subsides the party notices [[Kel]] run off to ensure his home, at the [[Side Quest Tavern]] is still intact. The party now turns to the forest and remembers all the refugee gnomes who were residing in the forest. They remember [[Melkor Fluffybottom]] and his family as they led the party to [[Old Duskwind]]. They think of the town, and wonder what shape all the building and the people are in. As these people have been dealing with strife and struggle for as long as much of the party has been present in the town.

[[Borin Duskrag]], [[Shandra Loomens]], [[Father Elias Vance]] are leading the meeting. 
[[Father Elias Vance]] is accusatory of these vagabonds running around town and would like to put them behind bars or set them afloat in [[The Scar]].

At the far end, a raised dais holds a wide table where three council members sit in tense silence, parchment and candles scattered before them.

### Opening Beat: Arrival & Suspicion

As the party enters, murmurs ripple through the crowd. Father Elias slams a cane on the floor and speaks:

Father Elias Vance:

> “So the so-called _heroes_ return. The sky quakes, the forest burns, and your trail is never far from it. Speak your piece—_what_ did you find beneath our feet?”

> _“You arrive just in time. The rest of us were trying to piece together what’s left of our home. But I’m sure your... _adventures_... took priority.”_

> “Sit. Speak. Convince us you’re not the cause of this calamity.”_

### Beat 2: Report to the Council (Skill Challenge)

This is the players’ chance to recap their findings, either honestly or tactfully. They’ll need to convince the council they’re valuable, not dangerous.

#### 🎲 Suggested Skill Uses (Need 3 successes before 3 failures):

- Persuasion – to appeal to Borin or the crowd
- Insight – to read council reactions and tailor responses
- Arcana/Religion – to explain what Ulthagos or the glyphs might mean
- History – to contextualize Zora’s actions or local lore
- Performance – to frame the story in a favorable light
- Deception – if they leave out risky or dangerous truths

> Failure consequences might mean they’re placed under watch, or only allowed to go with a town-appointed escort.  
> Success convinces the town they’re the best hope.

You can have the party explain:

- Zora is tied to Ulthagos, an ancient and dangerous entity
- She posed as a helpless scholar and fled the library
- The destruction of the forest seems tied to her escape
- They found powerful artifacts that might be linked to the destruction
### Beat 3: Opposition from Elias

If the players start gaining traction, Father Elias interrupts.

Father Elias (slamming the table):

> “You want us to _believe_ this was all the work of one woman with a long tongue and a magic book? You bring chaos in your wake. Perhaps it is not Zora we must fear—but _you_.”

#### Player response options:

- Call Elias out for fearmongering (Intimidation, Insight, or raw Charisma)
- Appeal to the crowd – ask for a show of trust (Performance or Persuasion)
- Present evidence (a relic, the glyphs, etc.)

### Beat 4: Shandra’s Conflict with Soffalie

Shandra Loomens (softly):

> “The forest trusted you, Soffalie. So did we. But something followed you into our sacred groves…”

Let this be a moment for Soffalie to either:

- Defend her actions (or those of the party)
- Apologize and ask for redemption
- Stand firm and say she did what she had to

Give this emotional weight. A good resolution here can win Shandra’s support.

### Beat 5: Borin’s Decision

After enough back and forth, Borin raises his hand for silence.

**Borin Duskrag**:

> “Enough. The town needs action, not argument. We can’t afford to send a militia into the wilds. If these outsiders _truly_ want to prove their worth… they’ll track this Zora and bring back answers—or her head.”

He turns to the party:

> “You want our trust? Then earn it. Find Zora. And if you’ve brought a storm with you… you’d better learn to steer it.”

### BEAT 6: The Sailor's Interruption

This happens right as the argument between the council and the party reaches a climax.

-  Setup:
A grizzled voice calls out from the back of the hall. People part as a sailor nobody recognizes steps forward. He tips his hat, his grin sharp.

- Dialogue Prompt:
"Pardon me, gents and ladies… but seems to me you got bigger fish to fry. While you lot are bickering, there’s a ship been lurkin’ in your harbor. Crew’s quiet. Too quiet. I saw ‘em sail out just after the ground shook… Might be they know somethin’."

-  Father Elias Vance vouches for him:
Elias nods grimly, gesturing to the sailor.
"This is Trask, a reliable man of the sea. If he says he saw something, I believe him."

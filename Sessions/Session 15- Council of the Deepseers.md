## Scene: The Spirit of the Hollow Grove
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XxEhuSJF780&list=PLtdXajwkBeaUqspXbthJkim-l0_-k3gxU&index=39


**Setting:**  
The air within the hollow grove thickens like syrup. The dim light fades to a dusky silver, and the sound of dripping water grows faint, replaced by the rhythmic beat of a heart — not one, but many, pulsing in time with the players’ breath. Then the mists gather and form the **shape of a wolf** (Sibuna’s aspect) or **a fox** ([[Elaris]]’s aspect), depending on the tone you want:

- **Wolf (Sibuna)** → somber, death’s honesty, the hunt for truth, seeing through deception.
- **Fox (Elaris)** → cunning, rebirth, the trickster’s test, wisdom hidden in play.

Both share glowing eyes — **liquid gold**, like sunlight through stormwater.

"Why do you travel together?"
	What is your purpose?
	Do you trust your companions?
	What are you willing to risk to save your friends?
	Why is this world worth saving?
	 
Have party close their eyes and point to the person they trust the most.
- 

The creature pads forward, sniffing the air, circling the party. When it speaks, it does not use words at first — its voice is felt in the chest, like emotion given sound. Then, a whisper, almost human:

> “Mortals… you trespass the hollow paths between gods and ghosts.  
> You drink of the memory-waters, yet you do not drown.  
> So tell me… what is your _intent_?  
> What do you seek at the bottom of the world?”

Let each player answer in character — this is the test. Their responses will echo, and the water around them will ripple and whisper their truths.


###  Success or Failure

You can resolve this with either pure roleplay or a **group Wisdom (Insight)** or **Charisma (Persuasion)** check (DC 16–18).

- **If they answer with clarity and truth**, the creature’s form steadies. Its eyes soften.
    
    > “Then you are not thieves of divinity. You are keepers of its burden.”  
    > It pads closer and presses its nose to the **Gate Stone**, inhaling deeply. The stone pulses.  
    > “This will call to hungers older than seas. Keep it hidden, until the time of parting.”
    
- **If they falter or lie**, the creature snarls and its form fractures into water and claws.
    
    > “The untrue are devoured by their own reflection.”  
    > The group must fight a **Water-Touched Guardian** (use **Guardian Wolf** or **Fox Trickster Spirit** stat block — CR 5–7, can shapeshift mid-combat).
    
###  Divine Reward
Regardless of how the encounter resolves, the players will receive a **token of the god’s will** — a **Bag of Stillwater** (like a **Bag of Holding**, but specifically for sacred stones).

> **Bag of Stillwater**  
> A small leather pouch lined with silver thread, perpetually damp. When an object is placed inside, its reflection remains visible shimmering on the surface of the pouch’s opening.
> 
> - Functions as a Bag of Holding (capacity 500 lbs, 64 cubic ft).
>  
> - However, it can also safely store _divine or cursed objects_ without resonance or interference.
>
> - Once per long rest, the bearer may whisper a single word into the bag to have it reveal an object’s _true intent_ (acts as _Identify_ or _Detect Magic_, depending on your choice).
>  


###  Divine Insight (Roleplay Hooks)

As the god inspects each member:

- **Vel’korr**:
    
    > “You bear the touch of Ulthagos… but beneath it, another hand claws for your soul. The Mistress of Ravens watches you still. Beware when her wings blot the sun.”
    
- **Picasso**:
    
    > “You are seen by the one below all tides. His dreams seep through you like ink in water. If you listen too long, you will forget which voice is your own.”
    
- **Faefi**:
    
    > “You are a reflection searching for its surface. When you find it, will you choose to breathe… or to drown?”
    
- **Thorne**:
    
    > “The forest remembers your tread, hunter. It will not forgive nor forsake you. There is balance yet to be made.”
    

The god concludes:

> “Go, children of the shallows. Carry what you must.  
> When the moons drown and the tides rise red, return the stones to the Hollow Gate.”

# Sea Rot
  
Welcome to [[Duskwind Landing]], a modest but lively port town on the western interior sea of [[The Scar]]. Though relatively new, built in the aftermath of the great upheaval, Duskwind has quickly become a haven for traders, sailors, and those who prefer to operate outside the reach of the law. It thrives on commerce, both legitimate and illicit, and is known for its pirate-friendly atmosphere, industrial grit, and deep-seated corruption.

The town itself is a mix of tightly packed wooden and stone buildings, cobbled streets stained with salt and soot, and the ever-present smell of the sea. By day, the fish market bustles with merchants yelling over one another, and by night, the taverns fill with off-duty sailors, mercenaries, and criminals looking for coin, drink, or trouble.

Off the shore, rising from the waves like a jagged sentinel, is Widow’s Rook, a massive rock formation. Despite its ominous name, it is a favorite spot for young romantics who picnic along the shore, basking in the sunset while the tide crashes against its base. Some say strange lights have been seen flickering upon it at night, but most assume it’s just the lanterns of passing ships or the tricks of the moonlit waves.

Recently, an eerie sickness known as Sea Rot has begun afflicting traders who leave the harbor, their skin blistering and their minds clouded by fever before they return to shore—or worse, are found floating dead in the tides. The town is tense, sailors whisper of curses, and trade is slowing. To make matters worse, a group of strange, secretive gnomes arrived around the same time the sickness began. They keep to themselves, speak in hushed tones, and seem to be watching something—though no one knows what. Some believe them to be harmless wanderers, while others insist they are the cause of the town’s misfortune.

Whatever the truth, Duskwind Landing is no longer the lively, cutthroat port it once was. Fear is creeping in with the tides, and something foul lingers in the air. What role you play in all this is up to you.

Early Game Document

Key Locations & NPCs

1. The Sunken Sail Tavern
    

- [[Horace Greentusk]]: Half-orc barkeep, tough but fair. Knows everyone’s business.
    
- Crime Boss: A local smuggler with a soft spot but a reputation to maintain. Spooked by Sea Rot and eager for someone to solve the problem for him. Gains trust through a high-stakes card game.
    
- Priest of the Tidemother: Wants to rid the town of the Sea Rot through faith and investigation. If players fail the card game, they can turn to him for information and aid.
    

1. Event: The Washed-Up Corpse
    

- The session kicks off as a young boy bursts into the Sunken Sail, gasping for breath, exclaiming that a body has washed up on the beach.
    
- Both the Crime Boss and the Priest react, standing up and heading toward the scene.
    
- The players must decide whether to follow them to the beach or stay behind.
    
- If they follow, they witness the corpse firsthand, seeing the signs of Sea Rot.
    
- If they stay, [[Horace Greentusk]] will provide the background on the disease and its effects on the town.
    

Paths Forward

- Talking to the Crime Boss: If the players choose to engage with the smuggler, they must prove their worth by playing a card game with him. Winning earns his trust and more information about recent missing shipments and strange happenings at sea.
    

- Dwarven roulette 6 beer mugs are placed on a table, one of them contains traces of goblin piss. Two opponents take turns in choosing and drinking one. The player who gets the piss-beer loses. No sniffing allowed.
    
- Mason 20gp buy-in. Roll 6d6. Tally number of die which rolled a 4 or better. Take those die which rolled a 4+ and re-roll them, tallying which ones rolled 4+. Repeat until no die rolls 4+ Total number of die rolls over 4+. This is your score. Compare scores, highest score wins 80% of the pot. 
    

- Talking to the Priest: The Priest will openly share his knowledge, describing his fears that this is no ordinary disease but something supernatural.
    
- Investigating on Their Own: If the players are skeptical of both figures, they may choose to ask around town, visit the docks, or search for other clues.
    

Setting the Hook

- Whether they win favor with the Crime Boss, aid the Priest, or gather rumors themselves, the players will uncover hints pointing to an offshore island where something—or someone—may be causing the Sea Rot.
    

Next Steps

- Once the players have gathered enough leads, they will need to hire a ship and crew to take them to the island, setting the stage for the next phase of the adventure.

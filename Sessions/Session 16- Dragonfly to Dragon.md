# Scene: Travelers
### Scene Notes
The travelers walking up to witness the great storm of the dragon [[Nilah]]. Seeming [[Parsus]] followers. Actually [[Zora Meadowlark]], [[Sibil Meadowlark]], and [[Gork]] under a seeming spell. They came from the town [[Saugeo]].

What will they say?
##### Scene type:
*Roleplay*
##### Setting: 
*Once they land on the [[Scalecrest Bluffs]]*
##### Music options:
- *Links to music*
## Setup
*The party finds a safe place to land halfway up the mountain.* 
*They continue to walk up the trail*

## Descriptions
The trail is narrow, slick with frost and edged by cliffs that plunge into endless fog. Above, lightning flickers, illuminating massive stone spires that jut through the cloudbank like ancient towers. The scent of ozone and petrichor fills the air.

Up ahead, you spot three figures winding along the path — a small group of pilgrims, cloaked in travel garb, each wearing a faint symbol of [[Parsus]]: a silver cloud stitched into blue cloth. They stop when they see you, surprised but not alarmed.
![[insights_session16.png]]
### Roleplay Goals:

This scene’s purpose is to:

- Introduce Zora’s **charisma and motive** without revealing who she is yet.
    
- Give the players information about the **storm**, **Nilah**, and the **Gate Keys** indirectly.
    
- Let clever PCs detect something off (Insight, Arcana, Religion, or Perception).
    
- Foreshadow Zora’s philosophy — that _freedom_, even dangerous freedom, is worth the cost.
### Dialogue Beats

**Zora (as Mira)**:

> “The sky is trembling tonight, don’t you think? My sister says it’s the breath of Parsus, calling the faithful to witness her power. But I think… it’s something older.”

If the party mentions Nilah:

> “Ah, the storm dragon. The guardian of the heights. They say she’s been here since before the Scar, older than the gods who name her protector. I wonder if she tires of guarding secrets she’ll never use.”

If they mention the storm’s danger:

> “You can’t reach the heavens without walking through a storm. That’s what faith is, isn’t it?”

If the conversation turns to the gods or Dithoria:

> “Dithoria? I’ve heard whispers. A god asleep beneath the waves, yes? Some say his dreams are what shake the tides. If he suffers, should we not wake him?”
## Participants
Likely highlights:
- [[Vel'Korr]]
- [[Kel]]
- maybe [[Thorne]] if they talk about [[Heaven's Hole]]
## Rewards
*The travelers will give the party a necklace of [[Parsus]] which will cast sleep when activated*. 
### Outcome Options

**If the party trusts her:**  
She offers information — a hint about [[Thalmyre]] (“If you truly seek answers, go where secrets breathe.”).

**If they see through her disguise but don’t attack:**  
She praises their perception and suggests they “may yet see eye to eye,” then departs peacefully.

**If they try to fight:**  
[[Nilah]]’s storm grows violently in response — the ground shakes, forcing both sides to scatter. It’s clearly not time for this confrontation yet.

- A strong **moral setup** before meeting Nilah.
    
- A **lead to Thalmyre** for the next Gate Stone.
    
- A subtle moment to show Zora’s **humanity and conviction** before she becomes a more dangerous antagonist.
